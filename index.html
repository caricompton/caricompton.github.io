What is the Victim-Witness Assistance Program?

The Victim-Witness Assistance Program is often unheard of until one becomes a victim of a crime. However, it's vital to recognize that victims and witnesses have a dedicated support network to help them navigate the complexities of the criminal justice process. There are three Victim-Witness Coordinators, two for General District Court and one for Juvenile and Domestic Court. Our primary focus is advocating for victims' rights and ensuring they have a voice in their pursuit of justice. When engaging with victims, our initial inquiry is, "What input do they have on the case?" So, what rights do crime victims possess, and what resources are available to them? The Commonwealth's Attorney

5 min watch

The Commonwealth's Attorney is responsible for prosecuting felonies, specific misdemeanors, and traffic offenses. Within this office, the Victim Assistance Program is established to provide support to prosecutors handling cases with victims and witnesses. Our primary role includes reaching out to and assisting victims, and then relaying crucial information to the prosecutors. The office is staffed with one Commonwealth Attorney, a Chief Deputy, and six Assistant Commonwealth Attorneys. This video showcases one of our courtrooms, featuring our Commonwealth Attorney and Chief Deputy handling an actual criminal case. The victim involved in this particular case is a juvenile, and seated within the gallery is our Victim-Witness Coordinator for Juvenile and Domestic Court.
